The Standard Model of Particle Physics

Overview

Type Statistics Mass Spin Charge
Developed during the late 20th century, the Standard Model of particle physics describes the interactions between the electromagnetic, weak, and strong nuclear forces, as well as the interactions between subatomic particles. Only the most elemental particles and forces compose the Standard Model. Although a cornerstone of quantum field theory and theoretically self-consistent, the Standard Model cannot explain all phenomena in the universe. The Standard Model tries to reduce all physical behaviors and interactions into a smaller set of theories and laws.
